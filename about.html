About Me

Prounouns: He/Him & They/Them

I'm known as Munsterlandr, and I am a furry and therian with autism and ADHD. I'm also part of a plural system; collectively, we call ourselves The Crew.

Nonhumanity

Though I am human in physical form, that just isn't me. My ideal form would not be human, but rather something with fur, dexterous paws, digitigrade legs, and a long ‒ and usually prehensile ‒ tail; beyond that, though, it flip-flops between a handful of forms, with certain traits appearing or disappearing. I usually stick with the term Therian, but also often use the term Otherkin as these forms often are quite detailed.

Previously, I had an SVG of a graph I made to explain em (as previously stated, they get complex), but it's kind of become outdated lol. I plan on making something that uses HTML and CSS to create the form graph instead, but in the meantime, here's a large blurb.

Forms of Mine:

  • Moth-Canid-Dragon Thing

    White fur, golden antennae, occasionally grey or black markings. Tail is long, prehensile, and has a funky thing at the end of it.

    Often more humanoid, but sometimes not. Often with four arms/forelimbs.

  • Amphimorpho

    Similar colour scheme to moth-like form, but gold pawpads and inner goop, as well as some black markings.

    Species is made by Tuxedodragon, check out their page on it here.

    Often more animalistic, but sometimes more humanoid. Antennae and four forelimbs often cross over from moth-like form.

  • Yinglet

    Frankly, haven't "spotted" this form's colour scheme. Definitely a smaller one zho.

    Yinglets are from zhe webcomic Out of Placers, which can be read here. Be warned it is for adult audiences.

    Size "felt" can fluctuate, feels more like my brain trying to map it to my physical body though. Moth-like antennae as zhe eyewhiskers shows up at times.

  • Fox-Opossum Hybrid

    Colours are somewhat of a blend of those of the two animals, and fluctuate often. This form is pretty rare nowadays for me to feel.

    Can be more humanoid or animalistic. The antennae and four forelimbs from the moth-like form often seep over.
